# Usage Guide

## Menu Bar

Once launched, DotCalendar lives in your menu bar with a grid icon. Click it to open the popover.

The popover shows:
- **Dot grid** — visual representation of your year progress
- **Progress bar** — percentage of the year completed
- **Last update** — when the wallpaper was last generated
- **Update** — manually regenerate the wallpaper
- **Settings** — open the settings window
- **Quit** — stop the app

## Settings

Access settings via the menu bar popover or by launching DotCalendar again (Spotlight, Dock, etc.).

### General Tab

| Setting | Description | Default |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Active Dot Color | Color of today's dot | `#E8483F` (red) |
| Light Mode Background | Wallpaper background in light mode | `#2D4976` (navy) |
| Dark Mode Background | Wallpaper background in dark mode | `#525252` (gray) |
| Launch at Login | Start automatically on login | Off |
| Show in Menu Bar | Show/hide the menu bar icon | On |

### About Tab

Shows app version, author info, and links to GitHub and X.com.

## Customizing Colors

1. Open **Settings** > **General**
2. Click any color picker to choose a new color
3. Click **Apply** to regenerate the wallpaper with your new colors
4. Click **Reset to Defaults** to restore original colors

## Wallpaper Behavior

- The wallpaper **auto-updates at midnight** (Asia/Singapore timezone)
- If your Mac was asleep at midnight, it updates on wake
- The wallpaper supports **automatic light/dark switching** — macOS handles it natively
- The wallpaper appears in **System Settings > Wallpaper** under your photos

## Accessing Settings

You can open settings in multiple ways:
- Click the menu bar icon > **Settings**
- Launch DotCalendar again via **Spotlight** (Cmd+Space, type "DotCalendar")
- Click the DotCalendar icon in the **Dock** (if visible)
- Use the URL scheme: `dotcalendar://settings`

The settings window always appears in front of other windows.
